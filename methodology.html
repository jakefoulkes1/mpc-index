Text corpus

Every Monetary Policy Summary and minutes publication of the Bank of England's Monetary Policy Committee from August 2015 to June 2026: 94 documents — 92 from the regular meeting cycle plus 2 special (emergency) meetings, the 10 March and 19 March 2020 Covid-19 decisions. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 corpus audit; 2026-07-11 special meeting.)

The window starts at the August 2015 regime change: from that meeting the Bank began publishing the policy summary and minutes on the day of the decision (“Super Thursday”). Meetings were monthly through 2015–2016 and moved to eight per year from 2017; the scraper probes every month and tolerates the months with no meeting rather than hardcoding the schedule. (pipeline/scrape/era.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Until August 2021 the Bank published the full minutes as a separate PDF, with only the summary on the HTML page; 52 of the 94 documents were therefore reconstructed from the Bank's own PDF minutes, and every document records whether its text came from HTML or PDF. (pipeline/scrape/backfill_pdf.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

The corpus was reconciled against the Bank's own sitemap (zero missing, zero extra within the window) and against the voting record. A document's published date is meeting_end + 1 day, applied uniformly; the five known one-day gaps against the Bank's voting sheet are individually logged, and joins between the two sources use a ±3-day tolerance. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11; 2026-07-11 validation join.)

Raw source texts are © Bank of England and stay in a local, gitignored cache; the repository publishes derived scores and SHA-256 hashes only, with links to the source pages. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-05.)

Voting record

The Bank's published MPC voting spreadsheet, parsed to one row per (meeting, member): each member's preferred Bank Rate against the decided rate. skew is the average of all voting members' preferred rates minus the decided rate, following Apel & Blix Grimaldi (2012, p.13, after Gerlach-Kristen 2004). The sheet's date column is the announcement date, confirmed against known meetings. (pipeline/build_votes.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Index construction

Draft — JF to revise

The index is Apel & Blix Grimaldi (2012), “The Information Content of Central Bank Minutes”, Sveriges Riksbank Working Paper No. 261, implemented as described in the paper from a retrieved copy (not reconstructed from memory); the verbatim term lists and page references are stored in pipeline/score/lexicon/abg_2012.json. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 A&BG baseline.)

Net Index = [ (#hawk / (#hawk + #dove)) − (#dove / (#hawk + #dove)) ] + 1   (paper p.10)

Range [0, 2]; 1.0 is neutral (the ratio's fixed midpoint, not a fitted value). A document with zero hits is defined as neutral. (pipeline/score/abg.py NEUTRAL_VALUE; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Counting rule, per the paper: the whole document is lowercased and tokenised (word tokens; no sentence splitting — the paper counts across the full text). Each adjacent adjective-then-noun pair (“higher inflation”) counts as one hawkish or dovish hit if the adjective matches one of the paper's stems by prefix; multi-word nouns (“oil price”, “cyclical position”) match as a following token sequence. The seven core nouns of the paper's main Net Index are used; the paper's four-noun robustness extension is stored but unused. Immediate adjacency in the adjective-then-noun direction is an interpretation of the paper's worked examples, logged as such. There is no negation handling — the paper has none. (pipeline/score/abg.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Lag convention: a meeting's minutes are published simultaneously with its own decision, so in every predictive use (the benchmark ladder's L3 and the Spec 2/Spec 3 tests) the tone feature for a meeting is the previous meeting's index. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 ladder; 2026-07-11 inference.)

Market benchmark

Draft — JF to revise

Sources. The Bank of England's published instantaneous OIS forward curve (the daily archive oisddata.zip, whose era files cover 2009–2015 / 2016–2024 / 2025–present, plus the separate “latest yield curve data” file updated daily — whichever is fresher wins), and SONIA from the Bank's Interactive Statistical Database (series IUDSOIA). (pipeline/market/ois.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 market benchmark; 2026-07-11 curve freshness fix.)

Implied change. The forward rate is read from the curve at meeting date + 3 days, linearly interpolated between the curve's whole-month maturity buckets, minus SONIA. The +3-day convention compensates for the fitted curve smoothing over the discrete jump that happens at a meeting; the bias was quantified before the convention was chosen, and a 2-week-average alternative was rejected because it would overlap the next meeting in the monthly-meeting era. One implementation serves the live call card and the historical backtest. (pipeline/predict/market_probs.py, LOCK_OFFSET_DAYS=3; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 smoothed-curve bias.)

Two-state ±25bp mapping. The market is assumed to price a single possible move in one direction, of fixed 25bp size: phike or pcut = clip(|implied change| / 25, 0, 1) in the direction of the implied change, remainder phold. A documented simplification — real market pricing can imply multi-directional or non-25bp moves. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 market_probs.)

Historical benchmark. For each of the 94 meetings, the lock date is announcement − 2 calendar days, walked back day-by-day to the nearest available trading day (capped at 10 days, else a hard stop; in practice zero walk-backs occurred). Output: data/market_history.csv. (pipeline/market/ois_history.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Freshness guard. A live lock refuses to write if the curve snapshot is more than 2 business days old. (pipeline/predict/lock.py assert_curve_is_fresh; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Evaluation

Draft — JF to revise

Benchmark ladder (L0–L4)

  • L0 — always hold (phold = 1), the naive baseline.
  • L1 — market-only: the two-state ±25bp mapping above, exactly as in data/market_history.csv. This is the bar every other model must beat.
  • L2 — ordered logit (proportional odds, 3 classes) on the market-implied change alone.
  • L3 — ordered logit on the market-implied change plus the previous meeting's tone index and vote skew (both lagged; see the lag convention above).
  • L4 — member-level simulation: a pooled dissent-state transition matrix (fit on strictly prior votes) blended 0.5/0.5 with the market-implied distribution per member, aggregated over 5,000 Monte Carlo committee draws (fixed seed 42).

Expanding window: for each evaluated meeting, models are fit on strictly prior meetings only, training from August 2015; evaluation runs from 1 January 2019 (62 meetings). Outcomes are coded by sign only — {cut, hold, hike} — with one shared classifier (the verb adjacent to “Bank Rate” in the decision text). (pipeline/ladder.py; pipeline/decision_label.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)

Scheduled-only rule. Headline scores use the 60 scheduled meetings only: at lock time (2 business days before an announcement) nobody could have pre-registered a forecast for an emergency meeting not yet known to exist. The 2 specials are scored separately as a robustness line, never blended in. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 scheduled vs special.)

Scoring rules. Three-class Brier score (sum of squared probability errors across {cut, hold, hike}; range [0, 2]) and log score −log p(actual outcome), with p floored at the clip constant 1e‑9 to avoid −log(0) for a confidently wrong call; the clip is published in data/ladder_v1.json as log_score_probability_clip. (pipeline/predict/score_outcomes.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11; 2026-07-11.)

Fallbacks. An ordered-logit window with under 10 training examples, fewer than 2 outcome classes, or a non-converged fit (two-part check, including a degenerate-parameter guard) falls back to L1's probabilities and is logged in data/ladder_v1.json's fallback_log — 11 of 62 L3 windows, all before the sample's first cut in March 2020. (pipeline/predict/ordered_logit.py; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 scipy entry.)

Statistical tests (Spec 2 and Spec 3)

Spec 3: OLS of the market surprise (actual minus market-implied rate change, basis points; data/surprises.csv) on the lagged tone index, Newey–West HAC standard errors with 4 lags, on the 91 scheduled meetings; also run with lagged skew added, and on a post-hiking-cycle fragility subsample (from 1 September 2023, n=23). Spec 2: a likelihood-ratio test of whether adding the lagged index improves an ordered logit of the three-class decision over the market-implied change alone, with a minimum-sample guard of 10 observations. (pipeline/inference.py → data/inference_v1.json; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 headline inference.)

Lock-and-score protocol

Before an announcement, pipeline/predict/lock.py freezes the market-only reference and the current index readings with the git commit hash and SHA-256 hashes of every input used; a human then fills in the point call and rationale, and the committed data/predictions/lock-* file is the pre-registered record — never modified afterwards, misses included. Locks refuse stale curve data (see the freshness guard above). (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 lock machinery.)

Limitations

Draft — JF to revise
  • Risk premia in forwards. OIS forward rates embed risk/term premia as well as rate expectations, so the market-implied probabilities are not pure forecasts. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 market benchmark.)
  • Sparse lexicon hits. Across the 94 documents, the median number of A&BG bigram hits per document (hawkish + dovish) is 2, with an interquartile range of 2 (Q1 = 1, Q3 = 3; min 0, max 12) — each document's index value rests on very few matches. (Computed read-only by pipeline/lexicon_sparsity.py from data/index.json; quartiles use the median-inclusive convention.)
  • Lexicon vintage and topic-vs-stance. The term lists are the paper's 2012 vintage, used unmodified; a match such as “higher inflation” reflects what the Committee discussed, which need not be the stance it took. (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 A&BG baseline.)
  • No negation handling. Per the paper, a phrase like “no case for higher rates” still counts its inner bigram; the paper's method has no negation rule and none was added. (pipeline/score/abg.py docstring.)
  • Smoothed-curve bias. The Bank's fitted OIS curve smooths over the discrete jump at a meeting date; the +3-day convention recovers only part of the understated move (quantified at up to a few tenths of a basis point on the 2026 curve, expected larger on steep historical curves). (DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11 smoothed-curve bias.)
  • Small n. 94 documents in total; 60 scheduled meetings in the ladder's evaluation window; 23 in the fragility subsample. (data/ladder_v1.json; data/inference_v1.json.)
  • The two specifications disagree. Spec 3 (OLS on the continuous surprise) is nominally significant on the full sample (p = 0.0316) but does not replicate post-September-2023 (p = 0.1422); Spec 2 (ordered logit on the discrete decision) finds nothing (p = 0.4918). Both are reported. (data/inference_v1.json; DECISIONS.md 2026-07-11.)
