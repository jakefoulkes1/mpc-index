MPC Communication Index

Does the tone of Bank of England communication carry information about the next rate decision beyond what markets already price? Every call is locked before the announcement, timestamped, and scored — including the misses.

What is this?

This project tests whether the tone of the Bank of England's MPC minutes says anything about the next Bank Rate decision that financial markets have not already priced in. Every minutes document since August 2015 is scored with a published hawkish/dovish dictionary (Apel & Blix Grimaldi, 2012), producing the communication index charted below. The bar to clear is the market itself: probabilities implied by the Bank of England's own OIS forward curve. Before each announcement a probabilistic call is locked and timestamped; after the announcement it is scored, and misses stay on the record permanently. A null result — the market already prices whatever the tone says — is itself the finding.

Standing caveat: the market benchmark (L1) is the bar. L2–L4 must show genuine skill above L1 to be worth anything — matching or beating L0 (always-hold) is not the test, since L1 already does that easily.
